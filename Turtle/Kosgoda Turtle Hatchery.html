KOSGODA TURTLE HATCHERY





About Kosgoda Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, also known as the Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project, is a well-known turtle conservation center located in Kosgoda, a coastal village in Sri Lanka. It is a popular tourist attraction and an important initiative dedicated to the preservation and protection of sea turtles.The hatchery plays a crucial role in conserving and rehabilitating sea turtles, as well as raising awareness about the importance of their conservation. The primary objective of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is to protect sea turtle eggs and ensure the survival of these magnificent creatures.The process at the hatchery begins with the collection of sea turtle eggs, which are vulnerable to various threats, including predation, poaching, and natural disasters. Local villagers and volunteers patrol the nearby beaches at night to locate and relocate the eggs to the hatchery's protected areas. This helps to safeguard them until they hatch and increase their chances of survival.Once the eggs are relocated, they are carefully buried in specially designated sandy pits within the hatchery. The temperature and humidity levels are monitored and controlled to replicate the natural conditions of a turtle nest. This ensures the best possible conditions for the eggs to develop.After an incubation period of approximately 45-60 days, the baby turtles begin to hatch. The hatchlings are carefully monitored by the hatchery staff to ensure their safe emergence from the nests. Once they have hatched, the hatchlings are kept in tanks for a short period, allowing them to gain strength and energy before being released into the ocean.The release of the hatchlings is an incredible sight to witness. Visitors and volunteers have the opportunity to participate in this remarkable event by helping to release the baby turtles into the sea. It is a moment of great joy and significance as the hatchlings embark on their journey into the vast ocean.In addition to its conservation efforts, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery also plays a vital role in educating the public about sea turtle conservation. They conduct informative sessions and presentations to raise awareness about the importance of protecting these endangered species. Visitors can learn about the different turtle species found in Sri Lanka, their life cycles, and the threats they face in the wild.The hatchery also serves as a rehabilitation center for injured or disabled sea turtles. Turtles that have been caught in fishing nets, suffered injuries from boats, or ingested marine debris are brought to the hatchery for treatment and rehabilitation. Once they have recovered, efforts are made to release them back into their natural habitat.The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery has made significant contributions to the conservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. By providing a safe environment for turtle eggs to hatch, raising awareness, and rehabilitating injured turtles, they are working towards ensuring the long-term survival of these magnificent creatures.

Turtle Conservation

The hatchery works tirelessly to protect turtles from threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and illegal trade. Through awareness campaigns, educational programs, and habitat restoration efforts, they aim to ensure a sustainable future for these magnificent creatures.

Supporting Conservation

By visiting the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, you contribute to their conservation efforts. Your support helps fund research, rescue and rehabilitation programs, and initiatives to raise awareness about turtle conservation.
