HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY


About the Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project dedicated to protecting sea turtles and their eggs. It serves as a sanctuary for various species of turtles, providing a safe environment for their eggs to hatch and for the young turtles to grow before they are released back into the ocean.At the hatchery, visitors can learn about the different turtle species, witness the hatching process, and even participate in the release of the baby turtles. It's a unique opportunity to contribute to the conservation efforts and experience the wonder of these incredible creatures up close.

The hatchery plays a crucial role in safeguarding sea turtle populations, as Sri Lanka is home to five species of sea turtles: the Green turtle, Loggerhead turtle, Olive Ridley turtle, Hawksbill turtle, and Leatherback turtle. These species face various threats such as habitat destruction, pollution, fishing nets, and poaching. The primary objective of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to protect sea turtle nests and ensure the survival of hatchlings. The hatchery collects eggs that are vulnerable to predation and human disturbance from the beaches and relocates them to a safe environment within the hatchery premises. This increases their chances of survival and prevents them from falling into the hands of poachers. Once the eggs hatch, the hatchery nurtures the baby turtles until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Visitors to the hatchery have the unique opportunity to witness the hatching process and participate in the release of the hatchlings, making it a popular tourist attraction. In addition to its conservation efforts, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery also conducts educational programs and awareness campaigns to educate the public about the importance of protecting sea turtles and their habitats. They aim to inspire environmental stewardship and encourage responsible tourism practices. Visiting the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery allows individuals to contribute to sea turtle conservation while learning about these fascinating creatures and the challenges they face. It is a rewarding experience for nature enthusiasts and anyone interested in promoting marine conservation efforts.

Here are some things you can do in Hikkaduwa:

  • Enjoy the Beaches: Spend time relaxing on the golden sandy beaches of Hikkaduwa. The main beach area is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying water sports like snorkeling and surfing.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the vibrant coral reefs and marine life by snorkeling or scuba diving. Hikkaduwa is famous for its underwater ecosystem, and you can witness colorful coral formations, tropical fish, and even sea turtles.

  • Glass Bottom Boat Ride: Take a glass-bottom boat ride to observe the underwater world without getting wet. You can see the coral reefs, fish, and other marine creatures through the transparent floor of the boat.

  • Visit Hikkaduwa National Park: Explore Hikkaduwa National Park, located just a short distance from the town. The park is home to a variety of bird species, reptiles, and mammals. You can take a boat ride along the lagoon and enjoy the scenic beauty.

  • Visit Turtle Hatcheries: Hikkaduwa is known for its turtle hatcheries where you can learn about the conservation efforts for sea turtles. You can observe baby turtles and even participate in releasing them into the ocean.

  • Visit the Tsunami Photo Museum: Gain insights into the devastating impact of the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami at the Tsunami Photo Museum. The museum displays photographs and artifacts that highlight the destruction caused by the tsunami and the recovery efforts.

  • Experience a Traditional Massage: Indulge in a relaxing Ayurvedic massage or spa treatment. Hikkaduwa offers several wellness centers where you can rejuvenate your mind and body.

  • Explore the Coral Sanctuary: Hikkaduwa Coral Sanctuary is a protected area where you can snorkel or dive to witness the vibrant coral reefs. It's a great opportunity to see a variety of coral species and marine life up close.
