BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY


About Bentota Hatchery

Bentota Hatchery is a renowned hatchery located in the coastal town of Bentota, Sri Lanka. It is a popular destination for visitors interested in learning about marine life conservation and witnessing the remarkable process of sea turtle nesting and hatching. The hatchery is dedicated to the preservation and protection of sea turtles, which are an endangered species globally. It plays a vital role in raising awareness about the importance of conservation efforts and the threats faced by sea turtles due to factors such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. At Bentota Hatchery, trained staff and volunteers work diligently to collect sea turtle eggs from nearby beaches, ensuring their safety and giving them a higher chance of survival. The collected eggs are carefully incubated in controlled environments to mimic the natural conditions found on the beach. This process helps protect the eggs from predators and provides a higher survival rate for the hatchlings. Once the eggs hatch, visitors have the incredible opportunity to witness the baby turtles emerging from their shells. The hatchlings are carefully handled by the hatchery staff and then released into the ocean during carefully planned and supervised turtle releases. These releases give the young turtles the best possible start in their journey and increase their chances of survival in the wild. Bentota Hatchery also serves as an educational center, offering informative tours and interactive programs to visitors of all ages. The knowledgeable staff provides insights into the different species of sea turtles found in the region, their life cycles, and the challenges they face. Visitors can learn about the hatchery's conservation initiatives and gain a deeper appreciation for the importance of preserving these remarkable creatures and their fragile habitats. In addition to its conservation efforts, Bentota Hatchery often collaborates with local and international organizations to conduct research on sea turtles and contribute to scientific knowledge about their behavior, biology, and conservation strategies.Overall, Bentota Hatchery is a significant institution in the conservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. It serves as a hub for raising awareness, promoting research, and facilitating the safe release of baby turtles into the ocean. By visitin the hatchery, individuals can contribute to the preservation of these magnificent creatures and help secure their future for generations to come.

Turtle Rehabilitation

The hatchery not only focuses on conservation but also provides care and rehabilitation for injured and sick turtles. Through veterinary assistance, nurturing, and reintroduction into the wild, they strive to give these turtles a second chance at life.

Education and Awareness

The Bentota Hatchery actively promotes education and awareness programs to empower local communities and visitors about the importance of turtle conservation. By raising awareness about the threats turtles face and the actions needed to protect them, they aim to create a more sustainable future for these magnificent creatures.
